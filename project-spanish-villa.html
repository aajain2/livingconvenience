A 6,200-square-foot Spanish Colonial revival in Atherton, restored to its original character while receiving a complete technology infrastructure built from the ground up. Every system was designed to disappear into the architecture, preserving the warmth of hand-plastered walls, terracotta tile, and aged timber beams.

Time with this home is akin to being welcomed into a private retreat. The automation was created holistically and evolved organically, the architecture always true to the original estate. Discreet sensors and concealed infrastructure are sensitive and authentic to the period detailing.

A full Lutron HomeWorks lighting system was threaded through existing conduit and plaster channels, with over 60 individually tunable zones calibrated to complement the home's warm palette. Circadian scenes shift automatically from dawn through evening, while landscape lighting on the courtyard, fountain, and olive grove operates on astronomical timers. Distributed audio spans twelve rooms and the outdoor loggia via Sonance architectural speakers, flush-mounted and color-matched to surrounding surfaces. A dedicated Sonos backbone allows independent zone control through wall keypads and voice.

Climate is managed by a Savant-integrated HVAC system with per-room sensors, ensuring consistent comfort across the thick-walled structure. Automated roller shades behind original wood shutters respond to sun position and interior temperature, reducing solar gain without altering the facade. An enterprise-grade Ruckus wireless network blankets the property, including the detached guest casita and pool house, with seamless roaming and a dedicated VLAN for all IoT devices isolated from the family's personal traffic.

The result is a home where every piece of technology serves the architecture rather than competing with it. Lighting, sound, climate, and security operate as one quiet system, felt but never seen, honoring three generations of the family's history in the house.
